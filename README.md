# Support-Wing-Kit
​S-wing Kit ​The S-wing kit is a supplementary Support Wing system characterized by wings or arms extending from any type of vehicle. ​Core Mechanism and Installation ​Lifting/Propulsion Units: At the tips of these extended wings or arms, mechanical units are installed to generate lift or thrust. These can be positioned on the roof (top)
belly (bottom), or sides of aircraft or any other vehicles, whether manned or unmanned.
​Subframe Integration: The wings or arms are secured using a subframe located on the opposite or offset side of the wing assembly.
​Non-Invasive Attachment: This subframe acts as an anchor for cables or sling bands, ensuring the wing/arm assembly provides a firm lifting or pressing force to the vehicle body. Anti-abrasion guards are utilized to prevent the cables or slings from scraping or cutting the vehicle's exterior. This allows the kit to be attached or removed without impacting the original structural integrity of the vehicle.
​Control Interface: The control system is installed as an add-on kit within the vehicle’s cockpit or managed by a remote/online operator.
​Safety and Performance Enhancements
​The implementation of this kit directly enhances the safety of life and property while boosting overall performance. Examples include:
​Aircraft: By installing the happy (S-wing) system on the upper fuselage with six arms extending outward—supported by a subframe on the belly and secured via cables with anti-slip guards—the aircraft gains STOL (Short Take-Off and Landing) and vertical landing capabilities. In the event of primary engine failure, the system prevents a crash, allowing for a safe glide and landing.
​Submarines: Featuring a permanently installed, concealed happy system. When deployed, the extending wings facilitate significantly faster diving or surfacing maneuvers.
​Tanks: Equipped with a permanent happy system to enhance mobility. In emergencies, such as a broken track, the system provides enough lift or thrust to allow the vehicle to continue gliding or moving forward.
